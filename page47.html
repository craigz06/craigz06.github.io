Hi Craig, funny you should ask, I was researching them not long ago, as my mom thought she might have a painting from the artist Messinger who was somehow related, but now don't think it's from the same artist. I think Messinger married into the family? From what I see on our family tree, Ilona Hana (Eleanor) Messinger (Mezei) born 1889, married your grandmother Manci's brother Arpad Klein. As you probably know, there were three Klein Brothers, Manci was the daughter of one of the brothers (pretty sure the name was Moshe), your grandfather Sandor and my grandfather Arnold (my mom's father) were Adolph's sons. It's a little confusing, there may have been some Mezei related name changes? I believe some of my research showed your grandmother using the name Mezei/Messinger at some point. My mother actually remembers many stories including about your grandparents getting together. My mom has an incredible memory, she stayed with us at the beginning of Covid and it gave me an opportunity to ask her a lot of questions. Apparently your grandfather went to Budapest from Acs for medical school and stayed with his uncle (Manci's father). That is when he got to know your grandmother better. Your grandfather lived in Budapest and the other two brothers were in Acs, which is close to Gyor where I was born. If you an invite to view our family tree, let me know. I think it's your daughter who also has an ancestry.com tree? I think she came up as a 23andme match to me and my mom - Stephanie? Take care and stay safe!


What do I know? I know their USA lives pretty well and have basic facts there. (Except that the state of Georgia does not collect their vital records in digital form, nor do they keep them at the state level. So I have no marriage records for your father and mother, except their first, nor your dad's death certificate, or your brother's birth certificate (except I do have that in hard copy – hmmm I can scan that in).) … … Anyway, I know where Sandor and Margaret lived with their little boys before emigration: their address was Maria Valeria 1. (That street is now called Apaczai Csere Janos Utca.) Your father's maternal grandparents lived in Budapest at Kossuth Lajos 7. These addresses were in a prosperous area of Budapest and were nice residences. (Alas, Sandor and Margaret's address today has that communist architectural aesthetic, and the other address is now commercial space). Sandor was born in Acs (Town), Komarom (District), Komarom-Estergom (County), Hungary, about 60 miles west of Budapest, near the Slovak border, and on the Danube River. At the turn of the 20th century, Komarom had a thriving Jewish community that was completely wiped out by the Holocaust and no one Jewish is left there now. I did read that Jews in Hungary today are very careful not to reveal their heritage. Sandor went to Budapest to study medicine. It was likely that he met his future wife there whilst visiting his uncle Samuel. It seems that he never returned to Acs, as he emigrated to Buffalo shortly after qualifying as a doctor, as Arpad was already in New York. Margaret was born in Kunszentmárton (Town), Kunszentmárton (District), Jász-Nagykun-Szolnok (County), Hungary, about 90 miles east of Budapest, and about 60 miles west of the Romanian border. During the period of her youth, there was a prosperous Jewish community there, with an emphasis on commercial trade with Budapest for agricultural products. The Jews established a school there that was known for its quality and educated Christians as well as Jews. A list of merchants from the town at the time of her birth show several Samuel Kleins, but no way to tell which one is actually her father. In any case, he relocated to Budapest at some point. I have found a Samuel Klein who died and was buried in Budapest in 1939 at the start of the war, and I have tentatively assigned his death to her father. In 1941 there were 202 Jews living in the town of Kunszentmárton. In June 1942, all of the Jews not yet killed were sent to Auschwitz, where 180 perished. There are also no Jews left in this town anymore; there were only 2 living there in 1965. Lastly, I know a little about the family of Arpad Kallos and his wife. Arpad was quite successful in Budapest as a young man. So, the growing anti-semitism, even before WWI in Budapest, probably drove him and your other family members out. He married a woman from the Messinger family, who came from Hódmezõvásárhely, but at least some of them moved to Budapest, which is probably where Arpad met Ilona. Ilona's sister Iren married Antal Roth, a newspaper and book publisher. Ilona's brother Sandor Messinger married an Ilonka Klein who was very likely your great aunt – and Sandor Messinger was an attorney in Szeged, a small city near Hódmezõvásárhely. One of Ilona's nieces, Margit Roth, married a Janos Schwimmer, and they lived until their death in Raleigh, North Carolina.

Suzie Wagner
