We look at a Lot with Two Septic Fields

location: forgot

date:August, 2022

Craig and Liz had always dreamt of owning a little piece of heaven tucked away in the mountains. The idea of a serene escape from the bustle of city life was enticing, and they finally decided to take the plunge. Armed with optimism and a list of properties from their realtor, they embarked on a journey to find their mountain retreat.

Their first impression, however, was far from the dreamy landscapes they had envisioned. As they turned off the main road and into the subdivision, their hearts sank. The entrance was surrounded by a makeshift trailer park, giving them an eerie feeling that this might not be the right place. Their doubts only grew as they ventured deeper into the subdivision. The small road leading to the property was full of hairpin turns and so narrow that they had to carefully maneuver their car around every bend. It felt like a never-ending labyrinth of trees and winding paths..

Arriving at their designated lot, they were met with a perplexing sight. There were no clear boundaries, and they couldn't quite tell where their property began and ended. An unfinished foundation loomed beside them, starkly contrasting with the pristine wilderness that surrounded it. It was a testament to abandoned dreams. As Craig and Liz explored further, their dogs misbehaved, their excitement apparently mirrored by their furry companions. Amidst the chaos, they stumbled upon a spot with an enchanting view of the valley below. It was breathtaking, and for a moment, they allowed themselves to dream once more.

With renewed hope, they contacted the realtor, asking for the plat to clarify the property boundaries. The truth, when it came, was a cruel blow. A significant portion of the land had an easement for a septic field, and it was situated right on their property. There was no room for another field, and the prospect of sharing their mountain paradise with a sewage system was disheartening.

As they left the property, a tattooed man on a run-down bike, who lived in a trailer next to the entrance, gave them a disdainful look. It was a look that seemed to say, "This place is not for you." In that moment, as they drove away from the mountain property that had so captivated their dreams, Craig and Liz knew that their search for the perfect retreat would continue. Sometimes, the most enchanting views can't overshadow the harsh realities that come with them, and they were determined to find a place that truly felt like home.
