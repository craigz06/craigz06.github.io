Employment History

Architecture, Engineering, Construction and Motion Pictures, some how all of the interests in my life found work and a living. My strongest skill was my drafting and engineering skills that were useful in many disiplines. Pictured here is my office at ADC with its custom designed furniture.

EMPLOYMENT

Pacific Interiors
1991-2010

As Vice President and Sales Manager I ran the office, kept the books and ended up being the top producing salesman landing contracts with Atlanta's largest contractors. I also managed the projects successfully and kept the company in the black until we all retired.

Craig Associates AIA
1989-1991

Starting my own archtectural firm my first major clients were Freshens Yogurt and Michael Maloney, a developer. I design built a tilt up steel frame house that was framed in one day.

Architectural Design Concepts

1983-1989

Partner and VP my work oversaw the creation and coordination of the working drawings and specifications for multi-family projects that evolved to a niche of interactive computerized information displays.

Nimslo 3D Research
1980-1983

I had been experimenting in the garage with 3D projection systems when I learned Nimslo had received $10m from Timex. Learned much about machine shop work.

Environmental Systems Corporation
1974-1976

ESC was producing state of the art laser instrumentation and doing field work in drift analysis. They needed a draftsman and illustrator to document their product line.

Storer Studios 1970-1971

Became interested in filmmaking while at UT and then quit school to try it out. I couldn't have had a better experience in the field but soon learned I would be working on a terrible '"Blackexploitation" feature length film.
